Pancreatic Cancer Awareness

What is Pancreatic Cancer?

Pancreatic cancer starts in the tissues of the pancreas, an organ located behind your stomach. It affects how your body digests food and controls blood sugar.

Common Symptoms

Symptoms can include jaundice (yellowing of the skin), weight loss, back pain, loss of appetite, fatigue, and dark urine. Often symptoms appear late, making early detection crucial.

Age Group Most Affected

Pancreatic cancer mostly affects people above the age of 50. The risk increases with age, especially after 65.

Who Is More Affected – Male or Female?

Pancreatic cancer is slightly more common in males than females, although the gap is not large.

Recovery and Treatment Options

Treatment depends on the stage and includes surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation. Early detection offers a higher chance of recovery.

Chance of Recovery

If detected early, there’s a 20-30% chance of long-term survival. Late-stage detection reduces survival chances significantly.

Types of Pancreatic Cancer

1. Exocrine Tumors: Most common; affects enzyme-producing cells.
2. Neuroendocrine Tumors: Less common; slower-growing and often easier to treat.

What Should You Do?

If you or someone close shows symptoms, consult a doctor immediately. Regular check-ups, healthy diet, avoiding smoking, and awareness are key to prevention and early detection.
